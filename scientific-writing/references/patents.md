# Patent and Invention Disclosures

Patent writing in industrial biotech bridges science and law. Your job is to provide the technical substance that IP counsel shapes into claims. Understanding patent structure helps you write better disclosures and support stronger patents.

## Invention Disclosure (Internal Document)

The invention disclosure is your first step — an internal document that captures the invention for the IP team to evaluate.

### Structure

#### 1. Title of Invention
- Descriptive and specific
- "Method for Producing Succinic Acid from Crude Glycerol Using Engineered Escherichia coli with Dynamic Pathway Regulation"
- Not "New Way to Make Succinate" or "Improved Fermentation Method"

#### 2. Inventors
- Everyone who contributed to the inventive concept (not just execution)
- Inventorship is a legal determination — list all potential contributors and let IP counsel decide

#### 3. Problem Solved
- What couldn't be done before this invention?
- What's the limitation of existing approaches?
- Frame as specifically as possible

#### 4. Description of the Invention
- What is the invention? Describe it clearly enough that a skilled practitioner could reproduce it
- What makes it novel? What's the inventive step?
- How does it work? Mechanism, process, composition
- Include specific examples with conditions and results
- Describe the broadest version AND the specific best embodiment

#### 5. Prior Art
- What's been published or patented that's closest to your invention?
- How does your invention differ?
- Be thorough — IP counsel needs this to assess novelty and write claims that avoid prior art

#### 6. Experimental Evidence
- Data that demonstrates the invention works
- Comparison to prior art methods/compositions
- Reproducibility evidence

#### 7. Potential Applications
- All uses you can envision, broadly
- Different fields of application
- This helps counsel write broader claims

#### 8. Public Disclosures
- **CRITICAL**: List any public disclosures (publications, presentations, posters, grant abstracts, conversations with non-NDA partners)
- Dates of each disclosure
- In the US, you have a 1-year grace period from first public disclosure. In most other countries, you have zero grace period.
- Filing before public disclosure is always preferred

## Supporting Patent Applications

Once IP counsel decides to file, you may be asked to draft technical sections or provide additional examples.

### Enablement
The patent must enable a person of ordinary skill in the art (POSITA) to practice the invention without undue experimentation. This means:
- Specific strains, plasmids, or genetic constructs
- Growth conditions, media compositions, temperature ranges
- Catalyst compositions and reaction conditions
- Analytical methods for confirming results
- Working examples with actual data

### Written Description
You must show you "possessed" the invention at the time of filing:
- Describe the invention with specificity
- Include enough detail to distinguish from prior art
- Cover variations and alternatives you've considered

### Claims Support
Claims define the scope of protection. Your technical input helps write stronger claims:
- **Broad claim**: "A method for producing succinic acid comprising culturing an engineered microorganism of the genus Escherichia in a medium containing glycerol wherein the ldhA and pflB genes are disrupted"
- **Narrow claim**: "The method of claim 1 wherein the microorganism is E. coli KJ134 ΔldhA ΔpflB ΔadhE ΔackA and the medium comprises crude glycerol at a concentration of 20-80 g/L"

Broader claims provide more protection but are harder to defend. Narrow claims are more defensible but easier to design around.

## Key Biotech Patent Considerations

### Sequence Listings
- Any patent claiming nucleic acid or amino acid sequences requires a sequence listing in standard format
- Plan for this early — it's tedious to compile retroactively

### Deposit of Biological Materials
- Novel strains may need to be deposited in a recognized culture collection (ATCC, DSMZ) before filing
- The Budapest Treaty governs international deposit requirements

### Provisional Applications
- A provisional application establishes a filing date with lower formal requirements
- You have 12 months to file the full (non-provisional) application
- Useful for securing priority when a publication or presentation is imminent

### Freedom to Operate (FTO)
- Before commercializing, ensure your process doesn't infringe existing patents
- This is counsel's job, but you can help by identifying relevant prior patents in your literature reviews
- Note: published papers are also prior art

## Writing Principles for IP Documents

- **Be exhaustive in examples.** More examples = broader claim support. Test your invention under varied conditions.
- **Describe alternatives.** "While the preferred embodiment uses *E. coli* KJ134, the method may also be practiced with *C. glutamicum*, *S. cerevisiae*, or other succinic acid-producing microorganisms" — this broadens the patent.
- **Quantify everything.** Ranges, specific values, comparison data.
- **Avoid absolute statements** ("always," "never," "all") unless you can defend them.
- **Mark confidential.** Invention disclosures are trade secrets until published. Mark every page.
- **Document date of conception.** Lab notebooks, timestamped electronic records, witnessed disclosures.
