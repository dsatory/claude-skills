# White Papers

White papers in industrial biotech occupy the space between a technical report and a marketing document. They establish thought leadership, frame a problem in a way that positions your organization favorably, and educate stakeholders who may become customers, partners, or funders.

## When to Write a White Paper

- To articulate a technical vision or strategic position
- To educate potential partners or customers about a capability
- To support business development with substantive technical content
- To frame a problem space before a solicitation is released (pre-positioning)
- To communicate a complex technical concept to a mixed audience

## Structure

### 1. Title and Framing
- Titles should promise insight, not just topic: "From Waste Glycerol to Platform Chemical: How Engineered Microbes Unlock the $4B Succinic Acid Market" rather than "Bio-Based Succinic Acid Overview"
- Include a 2-3 sentence abstract/teaser at the top

### 2. The Problem (25% of content)
- Paint the picture: what's broken, what's at stake, what's the cost of inaction?
- Use concrete numbers: market size, waste volumes, supply chain vulnerabilities
- Establish urgency without sensationalism

### 3. Why Current Approaches Fall Short (15%)
- Fair treatment of alternatives — strawmanning competitors destroys credibility
- Specific technical limitations, not vague dismissals
- Acknowledge what current approaches do well before explaining their ceiling

### 4. The Emerging Solution (35%)
- Your technical vision, grounded in data
- Clear enough for a business reader, deep enough for a technical one
- Use analogies for complex concepts: "Dynamic pathway regulation works like a thermostat — the cell senses its own growth phase and automatically redirects carbon flux from biomass to product at the optimal moment"
- Include a key figure or process diagram
- Reference supporting data without drowning in methodology

### 5. Evidence and Benchmarks (15%)
- Published results, preliminary data, case studies
- Comparison tables against incumbent approaches
- Honest about maturity level — don't oversell

### 6. Path Forward and Call to Action (10%)
- What needs to happen next?
- How can the reader engage?
- For partnership-oriented: what capabilities do you bring, what do you need?

## Tone

White papers walk a line between scientific credibility and accessible persuasion:
- More narrative than a technical report
- More substantive than a marketing brochure
- Authoritative but not condescending
- Visionary but grounded — claims should not outrun evidence
- Write as if explaining to a smart person in an adjacent field

## Length

Typically 5-15 pages. Shorter is better if the content supports it. If you need >15 pages, consider whether it should be a technical report instead.

## Design Considerations

White papers benefit from visual polish:
- Professional layout with clear headers and pull quotes
- 1-2 key figures that crystallize the central concept
- Callout boxes for key statistics or definitions
- Consistent branding without being a sales brochure
